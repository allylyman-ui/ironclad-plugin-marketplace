# Ironclad Forecasting Principles

## Ironclad Fiscal Calendar
- Q1: February 1 – April 30
- Q2: May 1 – July 31
- Q3: August 1 – October 31
- Q4: November 1 – January 31

## Forecast Category Definitions

### Commit (70%+ win probability)
You'd bet your paycheck on this deal closing this quarter. Evidence required:
- Value of Contract (VOC) confirmed — pricing has been discussed AND agreed on a Gong call
- Power sponsor is aligned and accessible
- Mutual close plan (MAP) exists and milestones are on track
- Technical validation is complete
- Procurement or legal is engaged
- Close date is customer-confirmed, not a default end-of-month date

### Best Case (50-70% win probability)
Fully qualified, closing plan exists, but known hurdles remain. Evidence:
- Strong champion identified and engaged
- VisionMatch confirmed (prospect agrees Ironclad solves their problem)
- Pricing may be discussed but not finalized
- MAP exists but some milestones haven't been hit
- Haven't reached Power yet, or competitive bake-off still in play

### Pipeline (early/uncertain)
Still validating. Multiple unknowns. Evidence:
- Early-stage conversations
- No MAP or mutual close plan
- No Power access
- Competitive landscape unclear
- No pricing conversation has occurred

## ML Number Construction
- Build bottom-up from individual deals, not top-down from target
- Need a deal-based path to the number
- Commit deals form the floor
- High-confidence Best Case deals add to the ceiling
- Create & Close pipeline should only be included based on personal historical success
- Round to nearest $10K

## Staleness Criteria (Deals to Dead Out)
- Stage 2 for 60+ days with no recent engagement → flag for dead-out
- Stage 3-5 for 90+ days with no recent engagement → flag for dead-out
- No engagement from prospect in 60+ days → flag regardless of stage
- Main contact left the organization → flag

## ARR Validation Rules
- If pricing was discussed on a Gong call, the discussed number should match the Salesforce ARR
- If pricing has NOT been discussed, the ARR should not be changed without a pricing conversation
- Any deal past stage 3 with $0 ARR needs immediate attention
- Upside ARR should be set if there's >20% deviation potential AND >$100K deal size

## Close Date Validation Rules
- If a specific signature/decision date was discussed on a Gong call, the Salesforce close date should match
- Default end-of-month dates (month/30 or month/31) are suspect — flag unless customer-confirmed
- The 50%+ transactability rule: more than half the tasks needed to close should be completable by the close date
- A 15th-of-next-month date may signal a pull-forward opportunity

## Stage Exit Criteria
- Stage 2 → 3: Problem identified, value articulated, Ironclad positioned as potential solution
- Stage 3 → 4: Technical fit validated, differentiated from competitors, champion identified
- Stage 4 → 5: Mutual close plan agreed, pricing presented, key stakeholders aligned
- Stage 5 → 6: Terms negotiated, legal/procurement engaged, verbal agreement from decision maker
- Stage 6 → 7: Signature obtained, awaiting processing
